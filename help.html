Romeo and Juliet Remix

This webpage essentially permits you to investigate how character connections played a role in Romeo and Juliet. Specifically, you get to set connections between characters as integer numbers, and see how over/under dependence between these characters' consequences have been represented in the text. To use this webpage, there are some basic instructions:


1. You begin by selecting all the characters you want to investigate. If you want to investigate the system of Romeo, Juliet, Nurse, and Capulet, then you choose them.

2a. You then fill out the connections between each character. You may make this a one-way connection (such as Juliet's connection towards the Nurse different from the Nurse's connection towards Juliet) or a dual connection. To enter the information, in each box type "[Character Name]:[Weight of connection]" with spaces between consecutive connections.

2b. In terms of the numbers the weights of the connections represent, it is entirely relative, meaning it doesn't matter if you put 10 & 15 or 100 & 105. With that being said, in terms of relative value, it can be used as follows: 30-40 negative/enemy relationship, 20-30 poor relationsip, 10-20 Decent/close relationship, and 0-10 extremely close/almost necessary relationship. The range is based on the spectrum of users.

3. Once the connections are filled out, you will receive a pop-up asking you to choose which two characters you want to find the connection between. If you want to investigate Romeo and Juliet, simply type "Romeo Juliet", with a space seperating them.

4. You are then presented with a graph where the shortest path between the two characters are highlighted in red.
